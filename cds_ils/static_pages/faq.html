What are the Library desk opening hours?

While the Library is open 24/7, you can find the library's desk (loans, returns, enquiries) opening hours here.

You can return books outside these opening hours by depositing them in the red box in the entrance.

How do I borrow books?

You can borrow books at the Library desk (bldg. 52-1-054) with your CERN card. Or, you can request books online on the Library Catalogue: search for the book and click on "request". The books will be delivered by internal mail to your office or kept for you at the Library desk.

How long is a loan duration?

The normal loan period is 1 week or 4 weeks, depending on the document type, and is renewable if nobody else has requested the document. To check your loans and renew them, see Your Loans page.

How many books can I borrow?

There is no maximum. Borrow as many as you want!

Can I return books by internal mail?

Yes, simply write "Library" as recipient on the envelope.

I returned my books but they still appear on my termination sheet. What should I do?

EDH termination sheets are not updated automatically. Please contact us to get it signed.

How do I renew my loans?

Go to Your Loans page.

How do I renew my loans for books borrowed from other libraries?

Interlibrary loans can usually be renewed. Reply to the recall email you receive at the end of the loan period and we will get back to you as soon as possible.

I cannot renew a loan. Could you help me?

For books that are reserved by someone else, a renewal is not possible. Please bring or send back the book to the Library.

You can check if an e-book is available or place a new reservation on the book.

I cannot find a CERN document I'm looking for, why is it not in the Library catalogue?

The CERN Library Catalogue includes only documents acquired by the Library (books, proceedings, journals and standards), if you are looking for CERN scientific publications such as preprints, published articles or theses written by CERN Authors or collaborations, please consult the CERN institutional repository CERN Document Server within the collection "CERN Articles & Preprints".

Non-CERN articles and preprints are generally not covered by CDS, which is focussing on CERN scientific output. Comprehensive searches for literature in High Energy Physics should be performed in INSPIRE.
